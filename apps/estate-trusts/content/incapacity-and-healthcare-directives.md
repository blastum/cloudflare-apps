# Incapacity and healthcare directives

Date: 2026-06-13

Educational overview only; not legal or medical advice.

## Terminology

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Term</th><th>Meaning</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Advance directive</td><td>Umbrella: any written instruction for future medical care when you cannot decide</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Healthcare POA / proxy</td><td>Names an agent to make medical decisions on your behalf</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Living will</td><td>Your written treatment preferences (end-of-life, life-sustaining care) — not a property will</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Combined form</td><td>Some states merge POA + living will into one "advance healthcare directive"</td></tr>

  </tbody>
</table>

## Healthcare POA vs living will

- Living will: you speak directly about specific treatments (CPR, ventilation, tube feeding, dialysis, comfort care, organ donation).<br>- Healthcare POA: you empower an agent for judgment calls, emergencies, and situations your living will does not address.<br>- Together: living will sets baseline; agent applies your values to the unexpected.

## Illinois specifics

Illinois treats health care POA and living will as separate statutes:

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Document</th><th>Statute</th><th>Witnesses</th><th>Scope</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Statutory health care POA</td><td>755 ILCS 45/4-10</td><td>One</td><td>Agent decides when you lack capacity</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Living will</td><td>755 ILCS 35</td><td>Two (physician cannot witness)</td><td>Terminal condition; death-delaying procedures</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Mental health treatment preference</td><td>Separate IDPH form</td><td>Per form</td><td>Psychiatric treatment preferences</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Illinois POLST</td><td>Practitioner orders</td><td>Physician completes</td><td>Serious illness; EMS visibility</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

If both health care POA and living will exist, the agent generally decides unless unavailable. Financial matters use a separate Illinois durable POA for property (755 ILCS 45 Article III) — see [financial-power-of-attorney.md](financial-power-of-attorney.md).

## Health Care Surrogate Act

When no valid advance directive exists, Illinois law may authorize a default surrogate decision-maker hierarchy for incapacitated patients without an agent. Having your own POA avoids relying on default surrogates.

## Operational checklist

- Execute state-appropriate forms while you have capacity.<br>- Name primary and successor agents; confirm willingness.<br>- Discuss values and living-will choices with agent and family.<br>- Give copies to agents, primary care physician, and hospital-on-admission.<br>- Carry wallet card with agent name and document location.<br>- Review after diagnosis, divorce/remarriage, or every ~10 years.
