# Trust taxation and generation-skipping transfer (GST)

Date: 2026-06-13

Federal rules from acquired IRS sources; Illinois has no separate GST tax. Educational only.

Illinois: grantor trusts generally do not file IL-1041; non-grantor trusts may owe IL income and 1.5% replacement tax. See [il-1041-instructions-summary](../documents/il-1041-instructions-summary.md).

## Estate tax and marital planning

- Bypass trust: uses first decedent's exclusion; not marital-deduction property; keeps growth out of survivor's estate.<br>- QTIP trust: marital deduction at first death with §2056(b)(7) election; QTIP assets included in survivor's estate at second death.<br>- Illinois: separate Illinois QTIP election on Form 700 since 2009; no DSUE portability for IL estate tax.

## GST tax mechanics

GST tax applies to transfers to skip persons (generally grandchildren and lower generations, or unrelated persons more than 37.5 years younger).

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Transfer type</th><th>Description</th><th>Typical reporting</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Direct skip</td><td>Gift or bequest outright to skip person</td><td>709 Sch. D (lifetime) or 706 Sch. R (death)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Taxable termination</td><td>Interest in trust ends, skip person becomes beneficiary</td><td>706/709 GST schedules</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Taxable distribution</td><td>Distribution from trust to skip person</td><td>Form 706-GS(D) or 706-GS(T) as applicable</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

## GST exemption and allocation

- Each individual has a GST exemption amount indexed with the estate basic exclusion (verify in Form 709 instructions for gift year).<br>- Allocate exemption on Form 709 Schedule D (lifetime) or Form 706 Schedule R (estate) to trust transfers.<br>- Automatic allocation rules may apply to direct skips and certain trusts unless taxpayer elects out on a timely filed return.<br>- Inclusion ratio: unallocated GST exemption → trust may be partially or fully subject to GST tax on future skip transfers.<br>- Dynasty trusts: long-duration trusts designed to hold GST-exempt property for multiple generations; Illinois prudent-period rule limits duration.

## Crummey and ILIT note

Gifts to irrevocable trusts (including ILIT premiums) may be present-interest gifts qualifying for annual exclusion if Crummey withdrawal powers are valid. GST allocation still required for skip-person beneficiaries even when gift tax is zero.

## NIIT

Estates and trusts may owe 3.8% net investment income tax (Form 8960) at low income thresholds vs individuals. See [irs-documents i8960 summary](../../irs-documents/documents/i8960-instructions-net-investment-income-tax-summary.md).
