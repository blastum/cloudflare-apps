# Estate planning document checklist

Date: 2026-06-13

Educational checklist for a coordinated estate plan. Illinois context where noted; adapt for other states.

## Tier 1 — every adult

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Document</th><th>Function</th><th>Illinois notes</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Last will and testament</td><td>Executor, guardians, probate asset distribution</td><td>Often pour-over will if RLT used</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Durable financial POA</td><td>Agent manages money/property if incapacitated</td><td>755 ILCS 45 Art. III statutory form</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Healthcare POA</td><td>Agent makes medical decisions</td><td>755 ILCS 45/4-10; one witness</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Living will</td><td>Treatment preferences in terminal condition</td><td>755 ILCS 35; two witnesses</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Beneficiary designation review</td><td>IRA, 401(k), life insurance, TOD/POD override will</td><td>Coordinate with trust sub-trust plan</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

## Non-legal but essential

- Letter of instruction (accounts, advisors, passwords policy — not a will substitute)<br>- Digital asset inventory<br>- Proof of identity and titles/deeds list<br>- Asset-location map for executor/trustee<br>- Location of originals (fireproof storage; tell executor)

## System view

Three transfer channels must align:

1. Title: will, trust, joint tenancy, TOD/POD.<br>2. Contract: beneficiary designations on retirement and insurance.<br>3. Tax: 706/709/1041, Illinois Form 700, K-1s.

Misalignment (e.g., IRA payable to estate, or house not in RLT) is the most common planning failure.
