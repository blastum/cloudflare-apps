## Probate and everyday trusts

### Revocable living trust (RLT)

The grantor's main during-life vehicle: holds titled assets, names successor trustee for incapacity, and at death often **splits into sub-trusts** (bypass, QTIP, children's trusts) per a formula in the instrument. Does not by itself reduce estate tax.

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Facet</th><th>Detail</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Also called</td><td>Living trust, revocable trust, inter vivos revocable trust</td></tr>
    <tr><td>A-B / A-B-C role</td><td>Parent document — sub-trusts spring from it at first death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>When created</td><td>During grantor's life; funded by retitling assets</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Revocable</td><td>Yes — grantor may amend or revoke</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Inter vivos / testamentary</td><td>Inter vivos</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Typical beneficiaries</td><td>Grantor during life; spouse and descendants after death per plan</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Primary purpose</td><td>Probate avoidance, incapacity continuity, container for tax sub-trusts</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Marital deduction</td><td>N/A while grantor alive; sub-trusts may qualify at death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Uses estate exclusion</td><td>No while revocable; bypass sub-trust uses exclusion at first death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>In survivor's estate</td><td>Grantor's share still in estate; survivor's own revocable share remains in survivor's estate</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Basis step-up</td><td>At each owner's death on assets in the trust</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Income tax</td><td>Grantor trust — income on grantor's Form 1040</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Crummey powers</td><td>No</td></tr>
    <tr><td>GST / dynasty</td><td>May hold GST-exempt sub-trusts if drafted</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Spendthrift</td><td>Limited while revocable; stronger in irrevocable sub-trusts at death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>See-through (IRA)</td><td>Poor IRA beneficiary — often accelerates payout; prefer individuals or conduit</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Key tradeoff</td><td>Privacy and control vs must fund and maintain; no estate tax savings alone</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

See [wills-and-pour-over.md](wills-and-pour-over.md).

### Testamentary trust

A trust that **does not exist until death** — created under a will or by a formula in an RLT when the grantor dies. Used for staged inheritances, spendthrift protection, and bloodline control.

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Facet</th><th>Detail</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Also called</td><td>Trust under will, sub-trust at death, children's trust</td></tr>
    <tr><td>A-B / A-B-C role</td><td>Bypass and QTIP are often testamentary sub-trusts of an RLT</td></tr>
    <tr><td>When created</td><td>At grantor's death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Revocable</td><td>N/A — becomes irrevocable at death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Inter vivos / testamentary</td><td>Testamentary</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Typical beneficiaries</td><td>Children, grandchildren, or spouse per terms</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Primary purpose</td><td>Control timing of distributions; creditor/divorce protection</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Marital deduction</td><td>Only if structured as marital/QTIP share</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Uses estate exclusion</td><td>Yes if funded as bypass sub-trust</td></tr>
    <tr><td>In survivor's estate</td><td>Depends on sub-trust type (bypass: no; QTIP: yes)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Basis step-up</td><td>At grantor's death when trust is funded</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Income tax</td><td>Simple or complex trust — Form 1041, K-1s to beneficiaries</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Crummey powers</td><td>No (funded at death, not by lifetime gifts)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>GST / dynasty</td><td>May be GST-exempt if exemption allocated on Form 706</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Spendthrift</td><td>Common — limits beneficiary creditors' access</td></tr>
    <tr><td>See-through (IRA)</td><td>Conduit or accumulation variants possible if drafted for SECURE Act</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Key tradeoff</td><td>Post-death control vs ongoing trustee and 1041 compliance</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

## Married-couple estate-tax trusts

These trusts matter when federal and/or Illinois estate tax is a concern. Illinois has a separate $4M exclusion that is **not portable** — bypass planning often matters even when federal tax is zero.

### A trust (survivor's trust)

In an **A-B-C plan**, the surviving spouse's **own** revocable trust — typically their half of marital property plus assets they always controlled. Not the same as the marital QTIP (C trust).

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Facet</th><th>Detail</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Also called</td><td>Survivor's trust, survivor's revocable trust, A trust</td></tr>
    <tr><td>A-B / A-B-C role</td><td><strong>A</strong> in A-B-C; in simple A-B plans the marital side may be A or QTIP</td></tr>
    <tr><td>When created</td><td>Often pre-exists as survivor's RLT; confirmed or funded at first death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Revocable</td><td>Yes — survivor retains full control</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Inter vivos / testamentary</td><td>Inter vivos (survivor's living trust)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Typical beneficiaries</td><td>Surviving spouse during life; then children/heirs per survivor's plan</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Primary purpose</td><td>Hold survivor's separate share without mixing with bypass or QTIP</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Marital deduction</td><td>No — this is survivor's own property, not a transfer from first decedent</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Uses estate exclusion</td><td>Uses <strong>survivor's</strong> exclusion at survivor's death, not first decedent's</td></tr>
    <tr><td>In survivor's estate</td><td>Yes — fully included</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Basis step-up</td><td>Step-up at survivor's death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Income tax</td><td>Grantor trust while survivor is alive and competent</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Crummey powers</td><td>No</td></tr>
    <tr><td>GST / dynasty</td><td>May pour to dynasty trusts at survivor's death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Spendthrift</td><td>Weak while revocable</td></tr>
    <tr><td>See-through (IRA)</td><td>Generally poor IRA beneficiary</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Key tradeoff</td><td>Full flexibility for survivor vs no use of first decedent's exclusion for this share</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

### B trust (bypass / credit shelter / family trust)

At the **first spouse's death**, a formula funds this **irrevocable** sub-trust up to the available estate tax exclusion. Assets and their growth are meant to **bypass** the survivor's taxable estate at the second death. The survivor often receives income and HEMS principal access but does not own the trust outright.

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Facet</th><th>Detail</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Also called</td><td><strong>B trust</strong>, bypass trust, credit shelter trust, family trust, non-marital trust, exemption trust</td></tr>
    <tr><td>A-B / A-B-C role</td><td><strong>B</strong> in both A-B and A-B-C plans</td></tr>
    <tr><td>When created</td><td>At first spouse's death (testamentary sub-trust of RLT or will)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Revocable</td><td>No — irrevocable once funded</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Inter vivos / testamentary</td><td>Testamentary (springs from RLT/will formula)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Typical beneficiaries</td><td>Surviving spouse (income + limited principal); remainder to children/heirs</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Primary purpose</td><td>Lock in first decedent's exclusion; shelter appreciation from second estate tax</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Marital deduction</td><td>No — intentionally <strong>not</strong> marital property</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Uses estate exclusion</td><td>Yes — funded to exclusion amount (federal and often IL)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>In survivor's estate</td><td>No — if no general power of appointment (HEMS invasion only)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Basis step-up</td><td>Step-up at <strong>first</strong> death only; <strong>no</strong> second step-up at survivor's death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Income tax</td><td>Complex or simple trust — Form 1041; DNI may pass income to spouse via K-1</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Crummey powers</td><td>No</td></tr>
    <tr><td>GST / dynasty</td><td>Remainder may be GST-exempt if exemption allocated</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Spendthrift</td><td>Strong for remainder beneficiaries; spouse access limited to terms</td></tr>
    <tr><td>See-through (IRA)</td><td>Generally <strong>avoid</strong> as IRA beneficiary — accelerates tax; prefer spouse or conduit</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Key tradeoff</td><td>Tax savings and creditor shield vs survivor cannot freely spend principal; admin after first death</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

See [ab-abc-trust-plan.md](ab-abc-trust-plan.md).

### C trust (marital QTIP)

In an **A-B-C plan**, holds the **marital share above the bypass amount**. Spouse gets income for life; first decedent controls who receives remainder (e.g., children from prior marriage). Requires a **QTIP election** on Form 706.

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Facet</th><th>Detail</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Also called</td><td><strong>C trust</strong>, marital QTIP trust, QTIP residue trust</td></tr>
    <tr><td>A-B / A-B-C role</td><td><strong>C</strong> in A-B-C (marital share after B is funded)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>When created</td><td>At first spouse's death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Revocable</td><td>No</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Inter vivos / testamentary</td><td>Testamentary sub-trust</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Typical beneficiaries</td><td>Surviving spouse (income for life); remainder per first decedent's terms</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Primary purpose</td><td>Marital deduction for excess over bypass + control remainder beneficiaries</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Marital deduction</td><td>Yes — with QTIP election on Form 706</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Uses estate exclusion</td><td>No — uses marital deduction instead</td></tr>
    <tr><td>In survivor's estate</td><td>Yes — QTIP included at second death (with second step-up on those assets)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Basis step-up</td><td>At first death; second step-up at survivor's death on QTIP assets</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Income tax</td><td>Trust 1041; income typically taxed to spouse if distributed</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Crummey powers</td><td>No</td></tr>
    <tr><td>GST / dynasty</td><td>Remainder may pass to GST trust if planned</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Spendthrift</td><td>Moderate — spouse income rights; remainder protected</td></tr>
    <tr><td>See-through (IRA)</td><td>Poor choice as IRA beneficiary</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Key tradeoff</td><td>Spousal support + remainder control vs full inclusion at second death</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

### Marital trust

Umbrella term for any trust (or outright transfer) that qualifies property for the **unlimited marital deduction** under IRC § 2056. In practice often a **QTIP** or outright bequest to a U.S. citizen spouse. In A-B planning, the marital side may be called A trust or C trust depending on plan design.

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Facet</th><th>Detail</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Also called</td><td>Marital deduction trust, marital share trust, spousal trust</td></tr>
    <tr><td>A-B / A-B-C role</td><td>Marital half of the estate — may map to A (outright/survivor) or C (QTIP)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>When created</td><td>At first death (or outright at death)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Revocable</td><td>No once funded as sub-trust; survivor's own RLT remains revocable</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Inter vivos / testamentary</td><td>Usually testamentary sub-trust or outright bequest</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Typical beneficiaries</td><td>Surviving U.S. citizen spouse</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Primary purpose</td><td>Defer estate tax at first death via marital deduction</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Marital deduction</td><td>Yes — if qualified (QTIP election for terminable interests)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Uses estate exclusion</td><td>No for the marital amount itself</td></tr>
    <tr><td>In survivor's estate</td><td>Yes for QTIP; outright marital bequest also in survivor's estate</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Basis step-up</td><td>At first death; QTIP assets step up again at second death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Income tax</td><td>Depends on structure — outright to spouse vs QTIP trust</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Crummey powers</td><td>No</td></tr>
    <tr><td>GST / dynasty</td><td>Indirect — marital deduction defers tax; remainder planning separate</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Spendthrift</td><td>Outright marital gift: none; QTIP: limited for spouse</td></tr>
    <tr><td>See-through (IRA)</td><td>Spouse as individual IRA beneficiary often better than marital trust</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Key tradeoff</td><td>Tax deferral at first death vs concentration of assets in survivor's estate</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

### QTIP trust

**Qualified terminable interest property** trust: surviving spouse must receive **all trust income** for life; no one else may receive principal during spouse's life. Executor elects QTIP treatment on Form 706. Gives marital deduction at first death while first decedent names remainder beneficiaries.

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Facet</th><th>Detail</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Also called</td><td>QTIP, qualified terminable interest property trust, terminable interest marital trust</td></tr>
    <tr><td>A-B / A-B-C role</td><td>Marital trust in A-B; often <strong>C trust</strong> in A-B-C after bypass funded</td></tr>
    <tr><td>When created</td><td>At first death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Revocable</td><td>No</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Inter vivos / testamentary</td><td>Testamentary sub-trust (common) or less often inter vivos QTIP</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Typical beneficiaries</td><td>Spouse (income for life); children or others as remainder</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Primary purpose</td><td>Marital deduction + control who gets assets after spouse dies</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Marital deduction</td><td>Yes — § 2056(b)(7) QTIP election required</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Uses estate exclusion</td><td>No</td></tr>
    <tr><td>In survivor's estate</td><td>Yes — included at second death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Basis step-up</td><td>First death and again at spouse's death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Income tax</td><td>Trust 1041; spouse taxed on distributed income</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Crummey powers</td><td>No</td></tr>
    <tr><td>GST / dynasty</td><td>Remainder can be GST-exempt with allocation</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Spendthrift</td><td>Remainder beneficiaries protected; spouse has income rights</td></tr>
    <tr><td>See-through (IRA)</td><td>Generally avoid as IRA beneficiary</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Key tradeoff</td><td>Blended-family control vs QTIP assets taxed in survivor's estate later</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

Illinois allows a separate Illinois QTIP election on Form 700.

### Clayton QTIP

A **funding formula**, not a different trust species: allocate the **exclusion amount to bypass (B) first**, pour the **balance into QTIP**. Adapts if estate size changes between drafting and death. The resulting marital trust is still a QTIP with the same tax traits.

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Facet</th><th>Detail</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Also called</td><td>Clayton election, formula QTIP, bypass-first marital plan</td></tr>
    <tr><td>A-B / A-B-C role</td><td>Defines how B and C (or marital QTIP) are funded</td></tr>
    <tr><td>When created</td><td>Formula executes at first death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Revocable</td><td>Formula in revocable RLT; sub-trusts irrevocable once funded</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Inter vivos / testamentary</td><td>Testamentary funding formula in RLT/will</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Typical beneficiaries</td><td>Same as bypass + QTIP combined</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Primary purpose</td><td>Automatically optimize bypass vs marital split</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Marital deduction</td><td>On QTIP portion only</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Uses estate exclusion</td><td>On bypass portion</td></tr>
    <tr><td>In survivor's estate</td><td>QTIP portion yes; bypass portion no</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Basis step-up</td><td>Bypass: one step-up; QTIP: two step-ups</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Income tax</td><td>Per sub-trust (bypass + QTIP)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Crummey powers</td><td>No</td></tr>
    <tr><td>GST / dynasty</td><td>Per sub-trust planning</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Spendthrift</td><td>Per sub-trust</td></tr>
    <tr><td>See-through (IRA)</td><td>Coordinate IRA beneficiaries separately from formula</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Key tradeoff</td><td>Flexibility vs requires skilled drafting and executor coordination</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

### Disclaimer trust plan

A **planning pattern**: spouse receives assets outright (or in a broad trust) and may **disclaim** within nine months so disclaimed property **funds a bypass trust**. Disclaimer must qualify under § 2518. Not a separate trust type — the funded trust is typically a B trust.

    <tr><td>When created</td><td>Bypass funded only if spouse disclaims after first death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Revocable</td><td>Bypass sub-trust irrevocable once funded</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Inter vivos / testamentary</td><td>Testamentary mechanism</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Typical beneficiaries</td><td>Same as B trust after disclaimer</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Primary purpose</td><td>Flexibility to see actual estate/tax law before committing to bypass</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Marital deduction</td><td>On non-disclaimed marital share</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Uses estate exclusion</td><td>On disclaimed amount routed to bypass</td></tr>
    <tr><td>In survivor's estate</td><td>Disclaimed/bypass portion: no</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Basis step-up</td><td>At first death on all estate assets</td></tr>

### Portability-only plan (not a trust)

Survivor inherits **outright**; executor elects **deceased spousal unused exclusion (DSUE)** on Form 706. Listed for comparison — not a trust type.

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Facet</th><th>Detail</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>

### CLT

**Charitable lead trust** — pays income to charity for a term; **remainder to family**. Reduces gift/estate tax on transfers to heirs in high-value estates.

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Facet</th><th>Detail</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Also called</td><td>Charitable lead annuity trust (CLAT), charitable lead unitrust (CLUT)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>A-B / A-B-C role</td><td>None</td></tr>
    <tr><td>When created</td><td>During life or at death</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Revocable</td><td>No</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Inter vivos / testamentary</td><td>Either</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Typical beneficiaries</td><td>Charity (lead); heirs (remainder)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Primary purpose</td><td>Fund charity now; pass remainder to family at reduced transfer tax</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Marital deduction</td><td>No (charitable lead, not marital)</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Uses estate exclusion</td><td>Remainder gift tax value reduced by charitable lead interest</td></tr>
    <tr><td>In survivor's estate</td><td>No for properly structured lifetime CLT assets</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Basis step-up</td><td>Carryover on lifetime funding</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Income tax</td><td>Grantor or non-grantor CLT — different charitable deduction timing</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Crummey powers</td><td>No</td></tr>
    <tr><td>GST / dynasty</td><td>Remainder to heirs may trigger GST planning</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Spendthrift</td><td>For remainder beneficiaries</td></tr>
    <tr><td>See-through (IRA)</td><td>Not typical</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Key tradeoff</td><td>Transfer tax efficiency vs complexity and charity commitment during lead term</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>
